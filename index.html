Global Economic Health

The Covid-19 Pandemic has caused a severe slump in the global economy in 2020. 1 year after suffering from the pandemic, most countries are well into the late stages of their respective national recovery plans. The aim of this visualization is to investigate the economic health of various regions by analysing 2 variables, unemployment rate and annual GDP growth of each country. We will study these factors by analysing the trends and relationship of these varaibles with the Economic Freedom of a country. We will use the scores of the various factors of economic freedom released for each country by The Index of Economic Freedom, which is an annual published by The Heritage Foundation, that ranks and scores the application of the principles of Economic Freedom at a societal scale across 184 countries.

The Factors of Economic Freedom

The Index covers 12 quantitative and qualitative factors of economic freedom. These factors can be split into 4 categories, Rule of Law (government integrity, property rights, judicial effectiveness), Government Size (government speanding, tax burden, fiscal health), Regulatory Efficiency (business freedom, labor freedom, monetary freedom), and Open Markets (trade freedom, investment freedom, financial freedom). Each factor contributes to the Economic Freedom score received by the country. The weight of each factor differes from country to country.

Unemployment Across the Globe

Unemployment is a global issue that plagues all countries. The rate of Unemployment has increased over the past few years due to a variety of factors such as the Covid-19 Pandemic, Automation of Jobs and Lack of Education. The Global Rate of Unemployment for 2020 was 6.47%, which is a large increase from the rate of 2019, which was 5.37%. This is largely due to the emergence of the Covid-19 Pandemic. In 2021, most countries have entered a recovery stage after facing 1 year of the pandemic. Regions such South East Asia, and Eastern Europe are reporting Unemployment Rates that are lower than the global average in 2020. Regions such as Africa and South America which hit badly economically are still reporting high rates on Unemployment. For example, South Africa is reporting a rate of 28.2% in 2021.

Unemployment Rate (%) for 2021 across the Globe

The Impact of the Factors of Economic Freedom towards Unemployment Rate

There is positive trend when observing the relationship between a countries unemployment rate and its scores for the factors of economic freedom. Countries that scored better for the different categories, generally have a lower unemployment rate compared to countries that do not.

Annual GDP Growth Rate (%)

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) is the total monetary or market value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country’s borders in a specific time period. As a broad measure of overall domestic production, it functions as a comprehensive scorecard of a given country’s economic health. An increase in GDP indicates economic growth, while a decrease indicates a slump.

The heatmap above shows the annual GDP growth rate for the past 10 years. There was a sharp fall in the growth rates across all regions in 2020. The only regions that had a postive growth rate were East and Central Asia. For 2021, most regions saw positive growth while only the Pacific Islands has a negative growth rate of -0.1.
